Sigma (summation) notation

Before discussing area, we need to discuss some notations.

The sigma notation is used to express sums in a clear, concise way.

For example, to write the sum

∑i=110i,\sum^{10}_{i=1}i,i=1∑10​i,

Approximating areas

In geometry, students compute the area of shapes. But, there are a lot of practical applications to computing the area under curves.

For example, consider the function fff below. AAA is the area under f(x)f(x)f(x) from on [a,b][a,b][a,b].

Now, how do we compute the area AAA?

It is difficult to compute the area, so we don’t. Instead, we can divide the area into shapes that we can calculate the area of.

One way to do it is to divide the area into rectangles, and find the sum of those areas. The sum of all the areas of the rectangles will serve as an approximation for the area under f(x)f(x)f(x) on [a,b][a,b][a,b].

There are two main ways to divide up a region into rectangles: left-endpoint approximation and right-endpoint approximation.
The left-endpoint approximation ensures that the left-side of the rectangles always intersect the function.

The left-endpoint approximation undershoots the area when the function is increasing.		 The right-endpoint approximation ensures that the right-side of the rectangles always intersect the function.

The right-endpoint approximation overshoots the area when the function is increasing.

Performing an approximation

  • First, we need to decide how many rectangles we want to use, and then break up our interval [a,b][a,b][a,b] into that many subintervals of equal size. Then, we denote the amount of subintervals as nnn.

More rectangles means more accurate approximation.

  • Then, we partition our region into equal-sized subintervals all of width Δx\Delta xΔx.
    Δx=b−an\Delta x= \frac{b-a}{n}Δx=nb−a​

    This divides our interval [a,b][a,b][a,b] into subintervals in the form
    [x0,x1],[x1,x2],[x2,x3],…,[xn−1,xn].[x_0,x_1], [x_1,x_2], [x_2,x_3], \dots, [x_{n-1},x_n]. \\[x0​,x1​],[x1​,x2​],[x2​,x3​],…,[xn−1​,xn​].

  • Finally, calculate the area of each rectangle and sum them up for our approximation. The areas of the rectangles differ depending on if we are using left- or right-endpoint approximation.

As noted, there are two ways to perform this approximation, which we will define below.

Area under the curve

In other words, the area under a function fff over an interval [a,b][a,b][a,b] is
lim⁡n→∞∑i=1nf(xi∗)Δx\lim_{n\to\infty}\sum^n_{i=1}f(x_i^*)\Delta xn→∞lim​i=1∑n​f(xi∗​)Δx

where fff is a continuous, nonnegative function.

Definite integral

The expression above can be expressed using what are called definite integrals.
lim⁡n→∞∑i=1nf(xi∗)Δx=∫abf(x) ⁣dx\lim_{n\to\infty}\sum^n_{i=1}f(x_i^*)\Delta x = \int_a^b f(x)\dd xn→∞lim​i=1∑n​f(xi∗​)Δx=∫ab​f(x)dx

If f(x)f(x)f(x) is a function defined on an interval [a,b][a,b][a,b], the definite integral of fff from aaa to bbb is given by
∫abf(x) ⁣dx=lim⁡n→∞∑i=1nf(xi∗)Δx\int_a^b f(x)\dd x = \lim_{n\to\infty}\sum_{i=1}^n f(x_i^*)\Delta x∫ab​f(x)dx=n→∞lim​i=1∑n​f(xi∗​)Δx

provided the limit exists.

If said limit exists, then f(x)f(x)f(x) is said to be integrable on [a,b][a,b][a,b], or is an integrable function.

Properties of the definite integral

  1. If the limits of integration are the same, the integral is just a line and contains no area.
    ∫aaf(x) ⁣dx=0\int_a^a f(x)\dd x = 0∫aa​f(x)dx=0

  2. If the limits are reversed, then place a negative sign in front of the integral.
    ∫abf(x) ⁣dx=−∫baf(x) ⁣dx\int_a^b f(x)\dd x = -\int_b^a f(x)\dd x∫ab​f(x)dx=−∫ba​f(x)dx

  3. The integral of a sum is the sum of the integrals.
    ∫ab[f(x)+g(x)] ⁣dx=∫abf(x) ⁣dx+∫abg(x) ⁣dx\int_a^b [f(x)+g(x)] \dd x = \int_a^b f(x) \dd x + \int_a^b g(x)\dd x∫ab​[f(x)+g(x)]dx=∫ab​f(x)dx+∫ab​g(x)dx

  4. The integral of a difference is the difference of the integrals.
    ∫ab[f(x)−g(x)] ⁣dx=∫abf(x) ⁣dx−∫abg(x) ⁣dx\int_a^b [f(x)-g(x)] \dd x = \int_a^b f(x) \dd x - \int_a^b g(x)\dd x∫ab​[f(x)−g(x)]dx=∫ab​f(x)dx−∫ab​g(x)dx

  5. Where ccc is a constant, the integral of the product of a constant and a function is equal to the constant multiplied by the integral of the function.
    ∫abcf(x) ⁣dx=c∫abf(x)\int_a^b cf(x)\dd x = c\int_a^b f(x)∫ab​cf(x)dx=c∫ab​f(x)
