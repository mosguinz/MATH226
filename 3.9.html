Taking derivatives of exponential functions

Let’s start with exe^xex.

If f(x)=exf(x)=e^xf(x)=ex. Is f′(x)=xex−1f'(x)=xe^{x-1}f′(x)=xex−1?

  • No. f′(x)≠xex−1f'(x)\neq xe^{x-1}f′(x)=xex−1.
    The power rule do not apply to exponential functions.
    So, what’s the derivative of exe^xex then?

Derivative of a natural exponential function

Given a function fff, if f(x)=exf(x)=e^xf(x)=ex. Then, f′(x)=exf'(x)=e^xf′(x)=ex.

f(x)=ex  ⟹  f′(x)=exf(x)=f′(x)\begin{aligned} f(x)=e^x &\implies f'(x)=e^x \\ f(x) &= f'(x) \end{aligned}f(x)=exf(x)​⟹f′(x)=ex=f′(x)​

For any function ggg, where f(x)=eg(x)f(x)=e^{g(x)}f(x)=eg(x), the chain rule applies.

f(x)=eg(x)  ⟹  f′(x)=eg(x)⋅g′(x)f(x)=e^{g(x)} \implies f'(x)=e^{g(x)}\cdot g'(x)f(x)=eg(x)⟹f′(x)=eg(x)⋅g′(x)

Let f(x)=ex2f(x)=e^{x^2}f(x)=ex2. Find f′(x)f'(x)f′(x).

f′(x)=ex2⋅ddxx2=ex2⋅2x=2xex2\begin{aligned} f'(x) &= e^{x^2} \cdot \diff{}{x}x^2 \\ &= e^{x^2} \cdot 2x \\ &= 2xe^{x^2} \end{aligned}f′(x)​=ex2⋅dxd​x2=ex2⋅2x=2xex2​

Let f(x)=8e2x+4f(x)=8e^{2x+4}f(x)=8e2x+4. Find f′(x)f'(x)f′(x).

f′(x)=8e2x+4⋅ddx(2x+4)=8e2x+4⋅2=16e2x+4\begin{aligned} f'(x) &= 8e^{2x+4} \cdot \diff{}{x}(2x+4) \\ &= 8e^{2x+4} \cdot 2 \\ &= 16e^{2x+4} \end{aligned}f′(x)​=8e2x+4⋅dxd​(2x+4)=8e2x+4⋅2=16e2x+4​

Let f(x)=xe2xf(x)=xe^{2x}f(x)=xe2x. Find f′(x)f'(x)f′(x).

Notice that the product rule applies here since xxx is being multiplied by the exponential part, e2xe^{2x}e2x.

f′(x)=ddx(x)⋅e2x+x⋅ddx(e2x)=e2x+x⋅2e2x=e2x(1+2x)\begin{aligned} f'(x) &= \diff{}{x}(x)\cdot e^{2x} + x\cdot\diff{}{x}(e^{2x}) \\ &= e^{2x} + x\cdot 2e^{2x} \\ &= e^{2x}(1+2x) \end{aligned}f′(x)​=dxd​(x)⋅e2x+x⋅dxd​(e2x)=e2x+x⋅2e2x=e2x(1+2x)​

Let y=ex2xy = \frac{e^{x^2}}{x}y=xex2​. Find y′y'y′.

Similarly, the quotient rule appiles here.

u=ex2  ⟹  u′=2xex2v=x  ⟹  v′=1∴y′=u′v−uv′v2=2xex2⋅x−ex2x2=ex2(2x2−1)x2\begin{aligned} u &= e^{x^2} &\implies u' &= 2xe^{x^2} \\ v &= x &\implies v' &= 1 \end{aligned} \\ \begin{aligned} \therefore y' &= \frac{u'v-uv'}{v^2} \\ &= \frac{2xe^{x^2}\cdot x - e^{x^2}}{x^2} \\ &= \frac{e^{x^2}(2x^2 - 1)}{x^2} \end{aligned}uv​=ex2=x​⟹u′⟹v′​=2xex2=1​∴y′​=v2u′v−uv′​=x22xex2⋅x−ex2​=x2ex2(2x2−1)​​

Taking derivatives of logarithmic functions

Let’s start with ln⁡x\ln{x}lnx.

  • Let y=ln⁡xy = \ln{x}y=lnx. Find y′y'y′.
    To differentiate with respect to xxx, we start by rearranging for xxx.

    y=ln⁡xey=eln⁡xx=ey\begin{aligned} y &= \ln{x} \\ e^y &= e^{\ln{x}} \\ x &= e^y \end{aligned}yeyx​=lnx=elnx=ey​

    Then, by implicit differentiation, we have that:
    ddx(x)=dydxey1=dydxey.\begin{aligned} \diff{}{x}(x) &= \dydx{e^y} \\ 1 &= \dydx{e^y}. \end{aligned}dxd​(x)1​=dxdy​ey=dxdy​ey.​

    Rearranging for dydx\dydxdxdy​, we have the following expression:
    dydx=1ey.\dydx = \frac{1}{e^y}.dxdy​=ey1​.

    Since x=eyx=e^yx=ey, we can substitute xxx into the expression above to get the derivative with respect to xxx.
    dydx=y′=1x\dydx = y' = \frac{1}{x}dxdy​=y′=x1​
