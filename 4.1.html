4.1 — Related Rates

When a quantity changes over time, its rate of change is given by its derivative.

If two related quantities are changing over time, then their rates of change (i.e., derivatives) are related as well.

For example, if a balloon is being filled with air, both the radius and the volume of the balloon are increasing, and are related. In other words, the rate at which the radius are changing is also related with the rate at which the volume is changing.

In this case, both radius (rrr) and volume (VVV) are changing with respect to time (ttt). As such, we denote the respective rates with the following:

  • dVdt\diff{V}{t}dtdV​ — the rate of change of volume (VVV) with respect to time (ttt).
  • drdt\diff{r}{t}dtdr​ — the rate of change of radius (rrr) with respect to time (ttt).

One can also express the variable VVV as a function of time. That is, V(t)V(t)V(t).

Similarly, the radius can also be expressed as r(t)r(t)r(t).

Since both of the rates are changing with respect to time, we say that dVdt\diff{V}{t}dtdV​ and drdt\diff{r}{t}dtdr​ are related.

A spherical balloon is being filled with air at a rate of 2 cm3/s2\text{ cm}^3\text{/s}2 cm3/s. How fast is the radius of the balloon is increasing when the radius is 3 cm3 \text{ cm}3 cm?

Recall that the volume of a sphere is given by:
V=43πr3V = \frac{4}{3}\pi r^3V=34​πr3

Since the balloon is being filled with air, volume (VVV) and radius (rrr) can be expressed as functions of time (ttt).

V(t)=43π(r(t))3V(t) = \frac{4}{3}\pi (r(t))^3V(t)=34​π(r(t))3

We want the rate of change of radius when r=3r = 3r=3. Therefore, we want the function r′(t)r'(t)r′(t).

In order to obtain r′(t)r'(t)r′(t), we first take the derivative of our volume equation.

V′(t)=43⋅3π⋅(r(t))2⋅r′(t)=4π⋅(r(t))2⋅r′(t)\begin{aligned} V'(t) &= \frac{4}{\cancel{3}} \cdot \cancel{3}\pi \cdot (r(t))^2 \cdot r'(t) \\ &= 4\pi \cdot (r(t))^2 \cdot r'(t) \end{aligned}V′(t)​=3​4​⋅3​π⋅(r(t))2⋅r′(t)=4π⋅(r(t))2⋅r′(t)​

Note that the expression
V′(t)=4π⋅(r(t))2⋅r′(t)V'(t) = 4\pi \cdot (r(t))^2 \cdot r'(t)V′(t)=4π⋅(r(t))2⋅r′(t)

can also be expressed with the notation:
dVdt=4πr2⋅drdt.\diff{V}{t} = 4\pi r^2 \cdot \diff{r}{t}.dtdV​=4πr2⋅dtdr​.

Since we are adding 2 cm3/s2\text{ cm}^3\text{/s}2 cm3/s of air into the balloon, that implies that the volume is increasing at a rate of 2 cm3/s2\text{ cm}^3\text{/s}2 cm3/s. As such, we have that:

V′(t)=2.V'(t) = 2.V′(t)=2.

Therefore,
4π⋅(r(t))2⋅r′(t)=2r′(t)=24π⋅(r(t))2=12π⋅(r(t))2\begin{aligned} 4\pi \cdot (r(t))^2 \cdot r'(t) &= 2 \\ r'(t) &= \frac{2}{4\pi \cdot (r(t))^2 } \\ &= \frac{1}{2\pi \cdot (r(t))^2} \end{aligned}4π⋅(r(t))2⋅r′(t)r′(t)​=2=4π⋅(r(t))22​=2π⋅(r(t))21​​

To get the rate of change when r(t)=3r(t) = 3r(t)=3, we simply substitute the value in:

r′(t)=12π⋅(r(t))2=12π⋅32=118π cm/s.\begin{aligned} r'(t) &= \frac{1}{2\pi \cdot (r(t))^2} \\ &= \frac{1}{2\pi \cdot 3^2} \\ &= \frac{1}{18\pi}\text{ cm/s}. \end{aligned}r′(t)​=2π⋅(r(t))21​=2π⋅321​=18π1​ cm/s.​

Process for solving related rates problems

  1. Try to write an equation that relates all of the variables we are dealing with.
  2. Then, differentiate both sides of our equation. This new equation will relate the derivatives.
  3. Substitute in the values and solve.

A plane is flying overhead at a constant elevation of 4000 ft4000\text{ ft}4000 ft. A man is watching the plane from the ground.

The plane is flying horizontally away from the man at a rate of 600 ft/s600\text{ ft/s}600 ft/s. At what rate is the distance from the man to the plane increasing when the plane is exactly 3000 ft3000\text{ ft}3000 ft horizontally away from the man?

Let sss represent the distance from the man to plane (ft\text{ft}ft).
Let xxx represent the horizontal distance from the man to plane (ft\text{ft}ft).

2(3000)(600)=2sdsdt.2(3000)(600) = 2s\diff{s}{t}.2(3000)(600)=2sdtds​.
