Evaluating limits using limit laws

Evaluating limits of rational functions

Okay, what about something like lim⁡x→∞−7x14+2x\displaystyle\lim_{x\to\infty}\frac{-7x}{14+2x}x→∞lim​14+2x−7x​? How the fuck do we evaluate that?

As x→∞x\to\inftyx→∞, it may appear as though f(x)=−∞∞f(x)=\frac{-\infty}{\infty}f(x)=∞−∞​. However, ∞∞\frac{\infty}{\infty}∞∞​ is indeterminate. As such, we need to come up with another plan!

In cases like this, where we want the limit of a rational function, we can divide every term by xnx^nxn, where nnn is the highest power of xxx in the denominator. Then, evaluate the limit.

So, in order to evaluate lim⁡x→∞−7x14+2x\displaystyle\lim_{x\to\infty}\frac{-7x}{14+2x}x→∞lim​14+2x−7x​, we first divide by the largest power of xxx in the denominator. In this case, it’s x1x^1x1.
lim⁡x→∞−7x14+2x=lim⁡x→∞−7xx14+2xx=lim⁡x→∞−714x+2\begin{aligned} \lim_{x\to\infty}\frac{-7x}{14+2x} &= \lim_{x\to\infty}\frac{\frac{-7x}{x}}{\frac{14+2x}{x}} \\[1em] &= \lim_{x\to\infty}\frac{-7}{\frac{14}{x}+2} \\[1em] \end{aligned}x→∞lim​14+2x−7x​​=x→∞lim​x14+2x​x−7x​​=x→∞lim​x14​+2−7​​

Then, we can use the limit laws and the known limit values to evaluate the limit.

lim⁡x→∞−7x14x+2=lim⁡x→∞−7lim⁡x→∞14x+lim⁡x→∞2=−70+2=−72\begin{aligned} \lim_{x\to\infty}\frac{-7x}{\frac{14}{x}+2} &= \frac{\displaystyle\lim_{x\to\infty}-7}{\displaystyle\lim_{x\to\infty}{\frac{14}{x}+\displaystyle\lim_{x\to\infty}2}} \\[2.5em] &=\frac{-7}{0 + 2} \\[1em] &=-\frac{7}{2} \end{aligned}x→∞lim​x14​+2−7x​​=x→∞lim​x14​+x→∞lim​2x→∞lim​−7​=0+2−7​=−27​​

We have that
lim⁡x→∞−7x14+2x=−72.\lim_{x\to\infty}\frac{-7x}{14+2x}=-\frac{7}{2}.x→∞lim​14+2x−7x​=−27​.

Compute lim⁡x→∞6x2−7x+39x+2\displaystyle\lim_{x\to\infty}\frac{6x^2-7x+3}{9x+2}x→∞lim​9x+26x2−7x+3​.

lim⁡x→∞6x2−7x+39x+2=lim⁡x→∞6x2−7x+3x9x+2x=lim⁡x→∞6x−7+3x9+2x=lim⁡x→∞(6x−7+3x)lim⁡x→∞(9+2x)=∞−7+09+0=∞\begin{aligned} \lim_{x\to\infty}\frac{6x^2-7x+3}{9x+2} &= \lim_{x\to\infty}\frac{\frac{6x^2-7x+3}{x}}{\frac{9x+2}{x}} \\ &= \lim_{x\to\infty}\frac{6x-7+\frac{3}{x}}{9 + \frac{2}{x}} \\ &= \frac{\displaystyle\lim_{x\to\infty}\(6x-7+\frac{3}{x}\)}{\displaystyle\lim_{x\to\infty}\(9 + \frac{2}{x}\)} \\[2em] &= \frac{\infty-7+0}{9+0} \\ &= \infty \end{aligned}x→∞lim​9x+26x2−7x+3​​=x→∞lim​x9x+2​x6x2−7x+3​​=x→∞lim​9+x2​6x−7+x3​​=x→∞lim​(9+x2​)x→∞lim​(6x−7+x3​)​=9+0∞−7+0​=∞​

Compute lim⁡x→∞7x−14x3+10x−13\displaystyle\lim_{x\to\infty}\frac{7x-14}{x^3+10x-13}x→∞lim​x3+10x−137x−14​.

lim⁡x→∞7x−14x3+10x−13=lim⁡x→∞7x−14x3x3+10x−13x3=lim⁡x→∞7x2−14x31+10x2−13x2=lim⁡x→∞(7x2)−lim⁡x→∞(14x3)lim⁡x→∞(1)+lim⁡x→∞(10x2)−lim⁡x→∞(13x2)=01=0\begin{aligned} \lim_{x\to\infty}\frac{7x-14}{x^3+10x-13} &= \lim_{x\to\infty}\frac{\frac{7x-14}{x^3}}{\frac{x^3+10x-13}{x^3}} \\ &= \lim_{x\to\infty}\frac{\frac{7}{x^2}-\frac{14}{x^3}}{1+\frac{10}{x^2}-\frac{13}{x^2}} \\[1em] &=\frac{\displaystyle\lim_{x\to\infty}\(\frac{7}{x^2}\)-\lim_{x\to\infty}\(\frac{14}{x^3}\)}{\displaystyle\lim_{x\to\infty}(1)+\lim_{x\to\infty}\(\frac{10}{x^2}\)-\lim_{x\to\infty}\(\frac{13}{x^2}\)} \\[2.5em] &= \frac{0}{1} \\[1em] &= 0 \end{aligned}x→∞lim​x3+10x−137x−14​​=x→∞lim​x3x3+10x−13​x37x−14​​=x→∞lim​1+x210​−x213​x27​−x314​​=x→∞lim​(1)+x→∞lim​(x210​)−x→∞lim​(x213​)x→∞lim​(x27​)−x→∞lim​(x314​)​=10​=0​

Evaluating limits of functions involving a radical

Compute lim⁡x→∞3x−24x2+5\displaystyle\lim_{x\to\infty}\frac{3x-2}{\sqrt{4x^2+5}}x→∞lim​4x2+5​3x−2​.

Okay, now what? There’s a fucking square root over the denominator. We can’t just divide the denominator by x2x^2x2.

For large values of xxx, we have that
4x2+5≈4x2=2∣x∣.\sqrt{4x^2+5}\approx\sqrt{4x^2}=2|x|.4x2+5​≈4x2​=2∣x∣.

Which means that we can simply divide the numerator and denominator by ∣x∣|x|∣x∣.
lim⁡x→∞3x−24x2+5=lim⁡x→∞1∣x∣(3x−2)1∣x∣4x2+5=lim⁡x→∞3x−2x4x2+5x2=lim⁡x→∞3−2x4+5x2=34=32\begin{aligned} \lim_{x\to\infty}\frac{3x-2}{\sqrt{4x^2+5}} &= \lim_{x\to\infty}\frac{\frac{1}{|x|}(3x-2)}{\frac{1}{|x|}\sqrt{4x^2+5}} \\ &= \lim_{x\to\infty}\frac{\frac{3x-2}{x}}{\sqrt{\frac{4x^2+5}{x^2}}} \\ &= \lim_{x\to\infty}\frac{3-\frac{2}{x}}{\sqrt{4+\frac{5}{x^2}}} \\ &= \frac{3}{\sqrt{4}} \\ &= \frac{3}{2} \end{aligned}x→∞lim​4x2+5​3x−2​​=x→∞lim​∣x∣1​4x2+5​∣x∣1​(3x−2)​=x→∞lim​x24x2+5​​x3x−2​​=x→∞lim​4+x25​​3−x2​​=4​3​=23​​

Here’s a switcheroo. The denominator is fine, so we can simply divide the numerator and denominator by xxx.
lim⁡x→∞x2+12x15−8x=lim⁡x→∞1xx2+12x15x−8x=lim⁡x→∞x2x2+12xx215x−8=lim⁡x→∞1+12x15x−8=−18\begin{aligned} \lim_{x\to\infty}\frac{\sqrt{x^2+12x}}{15-8x} &= \lim_{x\to\infty}\frac{\frac{1}{x}\sqrt{x^2+12x}}{\frac{15}{x}-\frac{8}{x}} \\ &= \lim_{x\to\infty}\frac{\sqrt{\frac{x^2}{x^2}+\frac{12x}{x^2}}}{\frac{15}{x}-8} \\ &= \lim_{x\to\infty}\frac{\sqrt{1+\frac{12}{x}}}{\frac{15}{x}-8} \\ &= -\frac{1}{8} \end{aligned}x→∞lim​15−8xx2+12x​​​=x→∞lim​x15​−x8​x1​x2+12x​​=x→∞lim​x15​−8x2x2​+x212x​​​=x→∞lim​x15​−81+x12​​​=−81​​
